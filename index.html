Renewable Energy

Renewable energy, which harnesses the power of natural and inexhaustible sources such as sunlight, wind and water, plays a vital role in addressing some of the most urgent environmental issues that we face in the present day, including global warming caused by greenhouse gas emissions, the vulnerability of energy supply and stability due to dependence on finite resources. According to International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA), renewable energy sources accounted for 28.4% of the global electricity generation in 2021. However, the pace of renewable energy deployment varies across regions and countries, depending on the availability of resources, the level of policy support, the cost of technologies, and the existing infrastructure. This visualisation will explore more about renewable energy and the sustainability.

In 2020, most of the countries around the world had reached an average of electricity accessibility above 80% of their population. However, there are still some countries that have less than 50% of their population, which mainly located in Africa. This may be due to the lack of infrastructure and poor technologies.

Global Overview of Electricity Source

~ Oceania

Oceania is the only continent that have NO country using nuclear power to generate electricity.

~ Europe

Europe is the continent that use the most nuclear power for generating electricity. It has 132 nuclear reactor with a total energy generation of 876,244 GWh which accounted for 25.6% of the electricity production and 9.8% of the total energy supply in 2021.

~ Africa

The only country in Africa that has nuclear power is South Africa. It has only 2 nuclear reactors which are located at the Koeberg nuclear power station, providing around 5% of the electricity production of the country.

Fossil Fuel

United Arab Emirates (UAE) has most of its electricity geenerated by fossil fuel . It is considered as one of the country containing the MOST natural gas reserves in the world. The UAE accounts for about 3% of the world's total natural gas reserves. However, it plans to generate half of its electrical energy from solar and nuclear by 2025.

Nuclear

United States (US) is the country that use the MOST nuclear power to produce electricity. According to the International Atomic Energy Agency, France is the third highest nuclear power electricity user in 2020. Meanwhile, France is in the top place when it comes to the share of nuclear power electricity in total electricity generation for the whole country. About 60% of the country's electricity is produced by nuclear power.

Renewable Energy

Iceland and Nepal both achieve a 100% source of electricity comes from renewable energy . Iceland is full of cheap renewable energy such as hydropower and geothermal. Meanwhile, Nepal is a landlocked country in Himalayas with abundant hydropower potential. The unique natural conditions of these two countries is the main reason why they are able to generate electricity 100% from renewable energy .

Analyzing the Trends in Energy Intensity Level across Top 20 GDP Countries: A Heatmap Analysis

Energy intensity level is a measure of how much energy is used to produce one unit of economic output. This measure is important for tracking improvements in the energy efficiency of an economy over time.

Iceland has a relative HIGH energy intensity level, indicating that the country's GDP is highly relies on energy consumption. On the other hand, countries like Switzerland and Ireland has very LOW energy intensity level. However, it does not necessarily imply that Iceland is inefficient or wasteful in its energy use, but rather that it has different energy needs and opportunities than other countries.
