Renewable Energy

Renewable energy is the energy that comes from natural sources that are constantly replenished, such as wind, water, sunlight, and geothermal heat. Renewable energy can provide clean, reliable and affordable electricity for various purpose such as cooking, lighting and heating. According to the International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA), renewable energy sources accounted for 28.4% of the global electricity generation in 2021, up from 26.4% in 2020. However, the pace of renewable energy deployment varies across regions and countries, depending on the availability of resources, the level of policy support, the cost of technologies, and the existing infrastructure. In this visualisation, we will explore more about the renewable energy and its sustainability.

2020 Global Electricity Accessibility per Country Population

In year 2020, most of the countries around the world had reached an average of electricity accessibility above 80% of their population. However, there are still some countries that have less than 50% of their population, which mainly located in Africa. This may be due to the lack of infrastructure and poor technologies.
